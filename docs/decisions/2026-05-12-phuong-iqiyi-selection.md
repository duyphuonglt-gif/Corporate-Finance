# Stage 2: Company Selection Memo — iQIYI Inc (IQ)

## Executive Summary

iQIYI Inc (Ticker: **IQ**, NASDAQ) is selected for comprehensive financial analysis due to its strategic positioning in the high-growth entertainment streaming sector, available regulatory data, and compelling operational metrics for ratio-driven investigation. The company's negative profitability combined with strong EBITDA margins presents a unique case study for understanding capital efficiency, leverage dynamics, and turnaround potential in a maturing market.

## 2. Selection Rationale

iQIYI was selected for three strategic reasons:

1. **Financial Structure & Profitability Dynamics:** iQIYI's paradoxical financial profile—strong EBITDA margins (50.4%) juxtaposed with negative net margins (-0.7%)—reveals the critical interplay between operating efficiency and capital structure. With 43.1% Long-Term Debt/Capital, this case illuminates how leverage and cost of capital can obscure operational strength. This is ideal for investigating DuPont analysis, capital structure optimization, and the essential distinction between operating vs. financial performance.

2. **Cross-Border Financial Complexity:** As a China-based ADR (1:7 ratio) listed on NASDAQ, iQIYI introduces critical dimensions of international corporate finance: foreign exchange exposure (USD/CNY volatility), regulatory divergence between SEC and Chinese standards, and consolidated financial reporting under U.S. GAAP. This complexity demands rigorous analysis of currency risk impact on financial ratios, reconciliation adjustments, and economic substance vs. reported results.

3. **Strategic Industry Insight:** Domain expertise in the entertainment/streaming sector enables deeper investigation of content monetization strategies, subscriber acquisition costs, and competitive positioning relative to Netflix, Disney, and Warner Bros. Discovery. This enriches the ratio analysis beyond generic financial metrics, enabling investigation of industry-specific efficiency measures (Revenue per Subscriber, Content Spend as % of Revenue) and their strategic valuation implications.

## 4. Preliminary Observations

Five testable hypotheses emerge from initial data review, grounded in entertainment industry dynamics and cross-border financial complexity:

1. **Content Cost Economics:** EBITDA margin of 50.4% paired with net margin of -0.7% signals that content acquisition dominates operating expenses. *Hypothesis: Content spend as % of revenue is structurally excessive relative to pricing power; operating leverage will improve only if content costs decline or subscriber monetization increases.*

2. **Revenue Stream Diversification (Membership vs. Advertising):** Stable EBITDA despite declining revenue suggests membership and advertising revenue contribute unequally. *Hypothesis: Shift toward lower-margin advertising revenue explains net margin deterioration; gross margin compression masks this shift, detectable through segment-level contribution analysis.*

3. **Local Currency Impact (USD/CNY):** Operations are denominated in CNY while financials are reported in USD. *Hypothesis: USD appreciation in 2025–2026 artificially depressed reported revenue growth; constant-currency analysis will reveal stronger underlying operational performance than headline -2.4% CAGR.*

4. **Market Entry & Competitive Intensity:** Revenue declined -2.4% CAGR while Netflix and Disney grew. *Hypothesis: Intensifying competition from Tencent Video and Youku eroded market share and pricing power; competitive pressure is not fully captured in traditional ratio analysis and requires subscriber-level and per-user metrics.*

5. **Country Risk & Regulatory Constraints:** China's regulatory environment directly affects cash flows, capital structure, and cost of capital. *Hypothesis: Regulatory risks on content, foreign ownership, and capital outflows inflate the cost of equity; conventional beta (0.19) significantly underestimates true cost of capital and discount rate risk.*

## 5. Ratio Categories Preview

Six ratio categories directly support the five preliminary observations:

- **Profitability Margin Analysis (Content Cost Economics):** Gross Margin, EBITDA Margin, Operating Margin, Net Margin—tracks margin compression across layers to isolate content cost burden vs. SG&A efficiency
- **Revenue Quality & Mix (Revenue Diversification):** Revenue per Subscriber (ARPU), Subscription Revenue %, Advertising Revenue %, Contribution Margin by segment—reveals whether membership or advertising drives profitability
- **Currency-Adjusted Growth (Local Currency Impact):** Constant Currency Revenue Growth, Revenue Trend (USD vs. CNY adjusted), Operating Leverage—removes FX distortion to assess true operational momentum
- **Competitive Positioning & Scale (Market Entry):** Revenue per Employee, Subscriber Growth Rate, Market Share %, Operating Expense Ratio—benchmarks efficiency and competitive health vs. Netflix, Disney, Tencent
- **Leverage & Cost of Capital (Country Risk):** Debt-to-Equity, Interest Coverage, Debt-to-EBITDA, Cost of Equity (risk-adjusted), WACC—quantifies leverage burden and incorporates country risk premium
- **Valuation & Distress Signals (Overall Risk Context):** Price/Sales, Price/Book, EV/EBITDA, Free Cash Flow Yield—contextualizes whether discount reflects fundamental weakness or regulatory/geopolitical mispricing
