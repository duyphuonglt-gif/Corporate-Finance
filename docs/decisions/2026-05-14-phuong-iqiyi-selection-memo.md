## Executive Summary

iQIYI Inc (Ticker: IQ, NASDAQ ADR 1:7) is selected for Stage 3–5 analysis based on five testable hypotheses grounded in entertainment industry dynamics, international capital structure complexity, and acute profitability deterioration. FY2025 results (ended Dec 31, 2025) reveal a critical inflection: net margin collapsed from +2.7% (FY2024) to **–0.75%**, while gross margin compressed 310 bps year-over-year. This paradox—EBITDA margin reportedly 50.4% vs. net margin negative—offers a substantive case for investigating capital structure burden, operating leverage, and revenue mix dynamics. Audited financials are available via SEC Form 20-F; all data is denominated in U.S. GAAP, enabling direct ratio analysis without reconciliation friction. The company's China-based operations, cross-border ADR structure, and regulatory exposure introduce measurable country risk and currency exposure—ideal for testing international corporate finance hypotheses.

## Background

iQIYI operates as a Chinese online entertainment platform (movies, TV dramas, variety shows, anime) with a secondary social engagement layer. The company is incorporated in the Cayman Islands, operates through Hong Kong and PRC subsidiaries (VIE structure), and trades on NASDAQ as an ADR. As of May 13, 2026, the stock trades at $1.18 (down 40.7% YTD), with a market cap of $1.1 billion and 962,959 thousand ADS outstanding.

The selection is motivated by five interconnected financial questions:

1. **Content Cost Economics:** EBITDA margin (50.4%) vastly exceeds net margin (–0.75%), implying content acquisition or SG&A burden. Revenue declined 6.6% YoY while cost of revenues rose to 78.9% of sales (from 75.1% prior year). Is content spending structurally excessive?

2. **Revenue Stream Diversification:** Stable EBITDA despite declining aggregate revenue suggests unequal contribution from membership vs. advertising. Has a shift to lower-margin advertising revenue eroded profitability?

3. **Local Currency Impact:** Operations are CNY-denominated; financials reported in USD. USD appreciated ~2–3% against CNY in 2025. Does constant-currency analysis reveal stronger operational performance than headline –6.6% revenue decline?

4. **Market Entry & Competitive Intensity:** Revenue CAGR (2023–2025) = –14.3%, while Netflix and Disney posted positive growth. Did intensifying competition from Tencent Video and Youku erode pricing power?

5. **Country Risk & Regulatory Constraints:** PRC Enterprise Income Tax (statutory 25%) and regulatory uncertainty on content/capital flows inflate the discount rate. Is the low beta (0.19 vs. S&P 500) sufficient to capture geopolitical risk?

## Method

**Data Source:** Form 20-F (audited by Deloitte Touche Tohmatsu; filed with SEC). FY2025 (ended Dec 31, 2025) and FY2024 (ended Dec 31, 2024) consolidated statements of income and balance sheet. Valuation metrics from Mergent Market Atlas (May 8, 2026).

**Analytical Approach:** 
- Gross, operating, and net margin progression (FY2023–FY2025) to isolate cost structure deterioration.
- Revenue and cost-of-revenue trend analysis to quantify pricing power and content cost burden.
- Effective tax rate (ETR) calculation (FY2024: 7.2%; FY2023: 3.9%) to inform cost of capital.
- Leverage metrics (Long-term Debt/Capital = 43.1% from Mergent) to assess financial risk.
- Beta (0.19) and implied cost of equity (with 3% China risk premium) to evaluate discount rate assumptions.

**Key Assumptions:** RMB/USD conversion = 6.99 (verified from 20-F footnotes); shares outstanding = 964,911 thousand ADS (Dec 31, 2025); risk-free rate = 4.0% (May 2026 10Y Treasury).

## Findings

**Margin Compression & Profitability Cliff:**
Net margin deteriorated 660 bps in two years (6.1% FY2023 → –0.75% FY2025). Gross margin (27.5% → 21.1%) indicates content cost as primary driver; SG&A remained flat (11.6% of revenue both years). Operating margin collapsed to 0.8%—essentially a break-even operation before tax and other expense.

**Cost Structure Dynamics:**
Cost of revenues rose from 72.5% (FY2023) to 78.9% (FY2025) of total revenues. Concurrent 6.6% revenue decline (FY2025 vs. FY2024) and stable absolute COGS (RMB 21.5B both years) imply fixed content library cost; variable subscriber/advertising monetization did not scale. Hypothesis: membership ARPU and/or subscriber base contracted.

**Tax & Capital Structure:**
Effective tax rate (7.2% FY2024) is well below statutory 25%, suggesting HK subsidiary structure (5–10% withholding rate). Debt/Capital = 43.1%; implied tax-adjusted cost of debt ~4.5%. Cost of equity (Beta 0.19 + Rf 4% + MRP 6% + China risk 3%) ≈ 8.1%. Preliminary WACC = 6.4–6.8%, but FY2025 loss (pre-tax: RMB –59.5M) implies negative earnings yield, risking covenant breach if leverage is high.

**Currency & Regulatory Risk:**
20-F explicitly notes VIE structure, PRC resident enterprise classification risk, and dividend withholding exposure. Beta 0.19 (very low) does not capture these tail risks.

## Implications

**Proceed with Stage 3 Analysis.** The five hypotheses are testable via ratio decomposition and segment-level investigation (if disclosed in 10-K). FY2025 loss is not disqualifying; it sharpens the analytical question: is this cyclical (content overinvestment, competitive pricing war) or structural (secular margin compression in streaming)?

**Stage 3 Priority:** Extract and validate balance sheet debt levels, interest expense, and depreciation/amortization to verify EBITDA margin claim and calculate true cost of debt.

## Limitations & Next Steps

**Data Gaps:**
- EBITDA margin (50.4%, per Mergent) is not explicitly stated in 20-F; requires Cash Flow statement depreciation/amortization figures.
- Interest expense is embedded in "Total Other Expense" (RMB 288.8M FY2025); actual cost of debt must be calculated from debt footnotes.
- Revenue mix (Membership % vs. Advertising %) is not broken out in consolidated financials; segment disclosure may be limited or require 10-K supplementary data.
- Subscriber count and ARPU trends are not in audited statements; may require investor relations materials or segment conference calls.
