# Stage 2 Company Selection Presentation — iQIYI Inc (IQ)

## **PRESENTATION STRUCTURE & TIMING**

**Total Duration: 3-5 minutes**
- Opening: 20 seconds
- Company Overview: 45 seconds
- Selection Rationale (3 reasons): 2 minutes
- Preliminary Observations: 1 minute
- Ratio Analysis Framework & Next Steps: 30 seconds

---

## **OPENING (20 seconds)**

### Script:
"Good morning. I'm presenting my company selection for Stage 2: **iQIYI Inc**, ticker IQ, trading on NASDAQ.

iQIYI is a Chinese streaming entertainment platform—think Netflix for Chinese content. What makes iQIYI interesting for financial analysis is not just what it does, but **how it makes money and how that money flows through the income statement**.

Let me walk you through why I selected this company."

### Key Points:
- Name, ticker, exchange stated clearly
- Brief business context (streaming, China)
- Hook: "interesting financial paradox" to prepare for next section

---

## **COMPANY OVERVIEW (45 seconds)**

### Script:
"**Quick snapshot:** iQIYI operates across mainland China and Hong Kong. They have 4,600 employees. In 2025, they reported **$3.9 billion in revenue** and an **EBITDA margin of 50.4%**—that's exceptional operational efficiency.

But here's the puzzle: despite that 50% EBITDA margin, **net profit margin is negative at -0.76%**. They reported a net loss of $29.5 million in 2025.

Additionally, they carry **43% debt-to-capital ratio** and their stock trades at a significant discount to book value. These dynamics create a compelling case study for capital structure analysis."

### Key Points:
- Revenue scale: $3.9B (relatable)
- EBITDA margin 50.4% (strong)
- Net margin -0.76% (paradox)
- Debt-to-capital 43.1% (moderate leverage)
- Market discount (valuation opportunity)

---

## **SELECTION RATIONALE (2 minutes)**

### **Reason 1: Financial Structure & Profitability Dynamics (40 seconds)**

### Script:
"**First reason—Financial Structure.**

EBITDA of 50.4% signals strong operational performance. But negative net margins reveal a **capital structure problem**. The company is operationally efficient but financially burdened.

This gap between operating strength and net profitability is the heart of DuPont analysis. We need to investigate:
- Are depreciation and amortization costs unusually high?
- Is interest expense from the 43% debt load suppressing net income?
- What's the relationship between capital efficiency and shareholder value?

This is an ideal case to show students how leverage can mask operational success—or undermine it."

### Key Points:
- EBITDA 50.4% ≠ Net margin -0.76%
- DuPont decomposition question
- Leverage impact on profitability
- Teaching value: capital structure matters

---

### **Reason 2: Cross-Border Financial Complexity (40 seconds)**

### Script:
"**Second reason—Cross-Border Complexity.**

iQIYI is a Cayman Islands holding company that operates through Variable Interest Entity (VIE) contracts in mainland China. That structure creates regulatory, currency, and valuation complexity.

Revenue is generated in **Chinese Yuan (CNY)**, but reported in **US Dollars (USD)** via a 1:7 ADR conversion. When the US dollar strengthens, reported revenue declines even if operational revenue is stable.

In 2025, revenue declined 2.4% year-over-year. The question: **How much of that decline is real operational erosion versus FX translation headwind?**

This teaches constant-currency analysis and highlights country risk premium adjustments to cost of capital."

### Key Points:
- VIE structure complexity
- CNY operations → USD reporting
- FX headwind example (2025 revenue decline)
- Constant-currency needed to isolate real growth
- Country risk premium concept

---

### **Reason 3: Strategic Industry Insight (40 seconds)**

### Script:
"**Third reason—Industry Expertise.**

I work in the entertainment streaming sector. That domain knowledge lets me dig deeper than generic ratio analysis.

iQIYI competes against Netflix, Disney, and Chinese rivals like Tencent Video and Youku. The competitive dynamics are intense. In the 20-F Risk Factors, we see advertising revenue declining—**down 8.2% in 2024 and a further 9.1% in 2025**.

This signals a **revenue mix shift**. When advertising (typically lower margin) grows faster than membership revenue, it compresses profitability.

Understanding iQIYI means analyzing:
- Subscriber acquisition cost vs. lifetime value
- Content spend as % of revenue
- ARPU trends (Average Revenue Per User)
- Churn rates and subscriber growth

These metrics are critical for valuation but invisible in standard ratio analysis."

### Key Points:
- Domain expertise advantage
- Competitive context (Netflix, Disney, Tencent)
- Advertising declining: -8.2% (2024), -9.1% (2025)
- Revenue mix shift impact
- Industry-specific metrics (SAC, LTV, ARPU)

---

## **PRELIMINARY OBSERVATIONS (1 minute)**

### Script:
"Based on initial data review, I've identified **five testable hypotheses**:

1. **Content Cost Economics**: At 50.4% EBITDA margin paired with -0.76% net margin, content acquisition is likely excessive relative to pricing power.

2. **Revenue Mix Shift**: The advertising decline we see in Risk Factors suggests a shift to lower-margin revenue, explaining margin compression.

3. **FX Impact on Growth**: The -2.4% revenue decline in 2025 may mask stronger CNY-denominated operational growth. Constant-currency analysis will reveal this.

4. **Competitive Intensity**: Market share loss to Tencent and Youku eroded pricing power. Subscriber and ARPU metrics will quantify this.

5. **Country Risk Premium**: PRC regulatory constraints and VIE uncertainty inflate the cost of equity. Beta of 0.19 likely underestimates true risk.

Each hypothesis maps to a ratio category we'll analyze in Stage 3."

### Key Points:
- Five hypotheses listed with brief explanation
- Each grounded in data or business context
- Transition to ratio framework

---

## **RATIO ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK & NEXT STEPS (30 seconds)**

### Script:
"To validate these hypotheses, we'll conduct analysis across **six ratio categories**:

1. **Profitability Margins** – tracks compression from EBITDA to net income
2. **Revenue Quality & Mix** – subscription vs. advertising contribution
3. **Currency-Adjusted Growth** – isolates FX from operational momentum
4. **Competitive Positioning** – revenue per employee, subscriber metrics
5. **Leverage & Cost of Capital** – incorporates country risk premium
6. **Valuation & Distress Signals** – Price/Sales, EV/EBITDA context

In Stage 3, we'll populate these categories with actual ARPU, subscriber data, and constant-currency metrics from the 20-F MD&A. That analysis will either confirm or refute our hypotheses."

### Key Points:
- Six categories stated
- Stage 3 roadmap clear
- Data sources confirmed (20-F MD&A)

---

## **CLOSING (20 seconds)**

### Script:
"In summary: iQIYI presents a **compelling paradox**—strong operational margins masking financial weakness. That paradox is driven by capital structure, competitive pressure, and currency headwinds.

By investigating iQIYI's financials through the lens of DuPont analysis, constant-currency growth, and industry-specific metrics, we'll understand not just *what* the numbers are, but *why* they matter for valuation and risk assessment.

Thank you."

### Key Points:
- Restate the paradox (memorable)
- Why it matters (learning value)
- Professional closing
