## 1. Company Overview

iQIYI Inc (Ticker: IQ, NASDAQ ADR 1:7) operates as China's largest online entertainment platform, offering movies, TV dramas, variety shows, and anime through a subscription and advertising-supported model. The company is incorporated in the Cayman Islands, operates through Hong Kong and PRC subsidiaries via a VIE structure, and trades on NASDAQ as an ADR. As of May 13, 2026, the stock trades at $1.18 (down 40.7% YTD), with a market cap of approximately $1.1 billion and 962,959 thousand ADS outstanding.

FY2025 results (ended Dec 31, 2025) reveal a critical inflection: net margin collapsed from +2.7% (FY2024) to **–0.75%**, while gross margin compressed 310 bps year-over-year. Cost of revenues rose from 72.5% (FY2023) to 78.9% (FY2025) of total revenues, while total revenue declined 6.6% YoY. The company's China-based operations, cross-border ADR structure, and regulatory exposure introduce measurable country risk and currency exposure — ideal for testing international corporate finance hypotheses.

## 2. Selection Rationale

iQIYI is selected for Stage 3–5 analysis because it combines three conditions that stress-test ratio analysis in ways a stable company cannot: (1) acute margin deterioration creating a gap between reported EBITDA (50.4%) and net margin (–0.75%) that demands decomposition; (2) a VIE/ADR structure that layers tax, currency, and regulatory risk onto an already-strained income statement; and (3) audited U.S. GAAP financials available via SEC Form 20-F, enabling direct ratio computation without reconciliation friction.

The paradox — operating income positive while net income turns negative — is analytically productive. It isolates capital structure burden (interest expense embedded in "Total Other Expense," RMB 288.8M FY2025) as the proximate cause of loss, and content cost escalation (COGS/Revenue +640 bps over two years) as the structural driver. Revenue CAGR (2023–2025) = –14.3% against positive growth at Netflix and Disney, signaling competitive pricing pressure specific to the PRC streaming market. Each of these conditions maps directly to a testable hypothesis in Section 4.

## 4. Preliminary Observations

Five directional hypotheses, each stated as an expected outcome with supporting rationale drawn from the 20-F:

1. **Gross margin will compress a further 100–200 bps in FY2026** because content costs were stable in absolute RMB terms (≈RMB 21.5B both FY2024 and FY2025) while revenue fell 6.6%, indicating fixed-cost leverage works against iQIYI on a shrinking topline. Until subscriber volume or ARPU recovers, content cost deleveraging will continue.

2. **Membership ARPU contraction — not subscriber count decline — is the primary driver of FY2025 revenue decline** because advertising revenue typically falls faster than membership in a downturn, yet aggregate gross margin compressed more than COGS-ratio expansion alone would predict. A pure volume loss would not produce the observed EBITDA/net-margin divergence at this magnitude.

3. **The constant-currency revenue decline is 4–5%, not the reported 6.6%**, because the USD appreciated approximately 2–3% against CNY over FY2025. The 20-F confirms RMB-denominated operations translated at a weaker rate at year-end; adjusting for this should narrow the headline decline materially.

4. **The effective tax rate understates true tax burden**, because the reported ETR (7.2% in FY2024, 3.9% in FY2023) reflects preferential HNTE rates available to key subsidiaries (Beijing QIYI Century, Shanghai Zhong Yuan) through 2026–2028. If those licenses are not renewed — a regulatory risk the 20-F explicitly flags — the ETR would revert toward the statutory 25%, increasing cost of capital by 150–200 bps.

5. **Beta (0.19 vs. S&P 500) materially understates iQIYI's true risk**, because it measures co-movement with U.S. equities, not exposure to PRC regulatory actions (content bans, data-security reviews) or VIE unwinding risk — tail risks that a CAPM-based cost of equity will systematically underprice. A China risk premium of 3% is added as an explicit adjustment in the WACC assumption.

## 5. Ratio Categories Preview

Four ratio families will be calculated in Stage 3, each tied to at least one hypothesis above:

**Profitability:** Gross margin, operating margin, net margin, and EBITDA margin for FY2023–FY2025. This three-year trend isolates whether margin compression is accelerating (content cost hypothesis) or plateauing (competitive pricing reaching equilibrium). After-tax operating income (= Net income + (1 − tax rate) × Interest expense) will be the numerator for ROA and ROC to strip out financing distortions.

**Leverage & Coverage:** Long-term Debt/Capital (currently 43.1%), Long-term Debt/Equity, Total Debt Ratio, Times Interest Earned, and Cash Coverage Ratio. These ratios will test whether the EBITDA-to-net-margin gap is structural (content costs) or financial (debt service). Covenant pressure assessment requires interest coverage to be above 1.0x — currently TIE = 0.25x, a red flag to quantify.

**Efficiency:** Asset Turnover (Revenue / Prior-Year Assets), Receivables Turnover and Average Collection Period, Inventory Turnover (using licensed copyright inventory). These ratios surface whether capital productivity has declined alongside revenue, or whether the asset base is still generating sales efficiently.

**Valuation & Market Performance:** Market Value Added (MVA = Market Cap − Book Equity), Economic Value Added (EVA = After-Tax Operating Income − Cost of Capital × Invested Capital), Market-to-Book Ratio, and Du Pont decomposition of ROE. With market cap at $1.1B vs. book equity of $1.9B, MVA is deeply negative; EVA will quantify the economic shortfall against a 9% assumed WACC.
